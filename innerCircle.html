☽ Behind The Curtain ☾

If you’ve found this hidden corner of the Wytches website, congratulations—or condolences, depending on how you look at it. You’re probably here because you want to know more about the world beneath the world, the one that scratches at the edges of your dreams and leaves strange footprints in the snow. I’m Seraphina Bellweather, and before you go any further, let me be clear: this path is not for the faint of heart.

The paranatural world is real. Magic is real. Aliens might also be real for all I know, but what I do know is that none of these things safe, or easy, or forgiving. The more you know about what lurks behind the veil, the more it knows about you in return. Curiosity is powerful, but it is also dangerous. Humans, for all our cleverness, are not built to handle raw magic or the things that slip through when the veil thins. Those “fluctuations” you hear whispered about? They are not opportunities, they are warnings. Hotspots, creatures, items—the stories are almost always cautionary tales.

If you truly want to set foot into this world, I can help you take the first step. Just know, once you’ve seen past the veil, there is no un-seeing it. There’s no returning to the world where shadows are just shadows and creaks in the night are only the wind. Caution, ritual, and respect are your best tools. Never stare down something you do not understand.

About our inventory: yes, most of what we sell at Wytches will work for its intended purpose; herbs, wards, stones, charms, and whatnot. But the true rituals, the ones that matter, have been purposefully scrambled, rewritten, and hidden for centuries by those who would rather keep humanity safe than powerful. If you’re frustrated by vague instructions, that’s by design. Safety first, always.

If you’re serious about learning more, move slowly, listen carefully, and never forget the rules. This world is as beautiful as it is deadly, and there’s no shame in turning back before you get in too deep. If you do decide to continue, I’ll do my best to keep you from getting lost.

Stay safe. Respect the unknown. And remember: never stare down something you do not understand
