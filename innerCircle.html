☽ Behind The Curtain ☾

If you have found this hidden corner of the Wytches website, chances are that you are looking to learn more about the world beneath/beside our own. It is the reality that scratches at the edges of your dreams and leaves strange footprints in the snow. My name is Seraphina Bellweather and I understand that drive... But let me be perfectly clear when I say that the path of knowing is not for the faint of heart and you do not have to walk it. You can turn around. It would be safer.

If you are still here, then I may as well confirm your deepest suspicions.

Magic is real.

Ghosts are real.

Werewolves are real.

The paranatural world is real.

Some of you will reach this page and not believe this.

Good. That's the safest option.

Knowing the truth is neither safe, nor easy, nor forgiving.

The more you learn about what lurks behind the veil, the more attention you tend to attract from the other side. Curiosity is what brought you here, and while it can be empowering, it is also dangerous. Humans are simply not equipped to handle raw magic or the entities that slip through the cracks and into our world. In truth, ignorance has always been our most powerful shield. Skepticism acts like a barrier, holding back forces that would otherwise flood into our world. Our ancestors understood this, and purposefully maintained doubt and uncertainty to keep us protected. Whilst some must know more in order to watch over the veil and maintain the boundaries, this number must remain low for us to stand a chance.

If you truly want to set foot into this world, I can help you take the first step. Just know, once you’ve seen the truth, there is no un-seeing it. There’s no returning to the world where shadows are just shadows and creaks in the night are only the wind. Caution, ritual, and respect shall become your best tools and you must never stare down something you do not understand.

If you’re serious about learning more, move slowly, listen carefully, and never forget the rules. This world is as beautiful as it is deadly, and there’s no shame in turning back before you get in too deep. If you do decide to continue, I’ll do my best to keep you from getting lost.
